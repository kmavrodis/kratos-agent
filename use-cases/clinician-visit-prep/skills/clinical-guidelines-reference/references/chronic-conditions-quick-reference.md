# Olympus Health — Clinician Quick-Reference

> Compiled from current public guidelines. Last reviewed 1 April 2026 by the Adult Primary Care Council. Owner: Dr. Beatrice Lin (Endocrinology). Reviewed quarterly.

Quote section numbers when citing in a brief. This is a quick-reference, not a substitute for the full source guideline.

## §1. Hypertension (NICE NG136 2025 update + ACC/AHA 2024)

**§1.1.** Confirm hypertension by **ambulatory or home BP monitoring** before starting treatment. Single clinic readings overestimate by ~10 mmHg on average.

**§1.2. Targets.**

| Population | Clinic target | Home / ABPM target |
|---|---|---|
| Adults <80 years | <140/90 | <135/85 |
| Adults ≥80 years | <150/90 | <145/85 |
| **Adults with type 2 diabetes** | <140/90 | **<130/80** (NICE NG136 §1.4.4) |
| Adults with CKD + albuminuria | <130/80 | <130/80 |
| Post-MI / established ASCVD | <130/80 | <130/80 (ACC/AHA 2024 §6.3) |

**§1.3. First-line therapy** for adults <55 (non-Black) or any age + T2DM: **ACE inhibitor or ARB**. For adults ≥55 or Black adults of any age: **CCB** (e.g. amlodipine).

**§1.4. Escalation.** If BP remains above target after 4 weeks of single-agent at maximum tolerated dose, add a second agent from a different class (ACE-i + CCB, or ACE-i + thiazide-like diuretic). Re-check at 4 weeks.

**§1.5. Resistant hypertension** = BP above target on ≥3 agents at optimal doses, one of which is a diuretic. Add spironolactone 25 mg if K⁺ ≤4.5 mmol/L; refer to specialist if K⁺ >4.5.

## §2. Type 2 diabetes mellitus (ADA Standards of Care 2026)

**§2.1. Glycaemic targets.**

| Population | HbA1c target |
|---|---|
| Most non-pregnant adults | <7.0% |
| Older adults, frail, limited life expectancy | <8.0% |
| Newly diagnosed, no comorbidity | <6.5% (if achievable without hypoglycaemia) |
| Pregnancy | <6.0% |

**§2.2. First-line pharmacotherapy** is **metformin** unless contraindicated (eGFR <30) or not tolerated. Start 500 mg daily, titrate to 1000 mg BID over 4 weeks.

**§2.3. Add-on with cardiorenal benefit.** For T2DM + ASCVD or T2DM + CKD (eGFR ≥20, or albuminuria), add an **SGLT-2 inhibitor** (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) regardless of HbA1c — outcome benefit is independent of glycaemia.

**§2.4. Add-on for weight + ASCVD risk.** For T2DM with established ASCVD or high risk, a **GLP-1 receptor agonist** (semaglutide, liraglutide) has outcome benefit.

**§2.5. Monitoring cadence.** HbA1c every 3 months until at goal, then every 6 months. eGFR + ACR at least annually.

## §3. ASCVD secondary prevention (ACC/AHA 2024)

**§3.1. LDL target post-MI / post-revascularisation: <70 mg/dL** (1.8 mmol/L). For very high risk (recurrent event, multivessel disease, FH), consider **<55 mg/dL** (1.4 mmol/L).

**§3.2. Statin intensity.** All post-MI patients should be on **high-intensity statin** unless contraindicated: atorvastatin 40-80 mg or rosuvastatin 20-40 mg.

**§3.3. Add-on if LDL above goal on max-tolerated statin:**

1. **Ezetimibe** 10 mg daily — first add-on, ~20% additional LDL reduction
2. **PCSK9 inhibitor** (evolocumab, alirocumab) — if still above goal after ezetimibe, especially for FH or recurrent events
3. **Bempedoic acid** — alternative if statin-intolerant

**§3.4. Antiplatelet.** Aspirin 81 mg daily indefinitely post-MI. DAPT (aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor) for 12 months after PCI, then aspirin alone.

**§3.5. Beta-blocker.** Indicated for ≥3 years post-MI; consider continuing indefinitely if LVEF <40% or recurrent ischaemia.

## §4. Chronic kidney disease (KDIGO 2024)

**§4.1. Staging.** By eGFR (mL/min/1.73m²):

| Stage | eGFR | Description |
|---|---|---|
| G1 | ≥90 | Normal, with kidney damage marker |
| G2 | 60-89 | Mildly reduced |
| **G3a** | **45-59** | **Mild to moderate** |
| **G3b** | **30-44** | **Moderate to severe** |
| G4 | 15-29 | Severe — nephrology referral |
| G5 | <15 | Kidney failure — RRT discussion |

**§4.2. Drug dose adjustment by eGFR.**

- **Metformin** — full dose if eGFR ≥45. Reduce to 1000 mg/day max if eGFR 30-44. **Stop** if eGFR <30.
- **NSAIDs** — avoid in eGFR <60 in T2DM, avoid in eGFR <30 generally.
- **ACE-i / ARB** — continue through CKD; expect a 20-30% eGFR drop on initiation that is *not* a reason to stop.
- **SGLT-2 inhibitors** — start down to eGFR 20; continue once started even if eGFR drops below 20.

**§4.3. Monitoring.** eGFR + ACR at least annually for everyone; every 6 months for G3a; every 3 months for G3b+.

## §5. Lipid management (NICE NG238 2025 + ACC/AHA 2024)

**§5.1. Primary prevention.** Calculate 10-year CV risk (QRISK3 or ASCVD risk calculator) before starting a statin. Offer high-intensity statin if 10-year risk ≥10%.

**§5.2. Targets** (see §3 for secondary prevention).

| Population | LDL target |
|---|---|
| Primary prevention | ≥40% reduction from baseline |
| ASCVD / post-MI | <70 mg/dL (§3.1) |
| Very high risk | <55 mg/dL (§3.1) |

**§5.3. Routine lipid check** 3 months after starting/changing therapy, then annually if at goal.

## §6. Asthma — adult (GINA 2026)

**§6.1.** Symptom control assessment at every visit: nocturnal waking, rescue inhaler use, activity limitation, daytime symptoms in past 4 weeks.

**§6.2. Stepwise therapy** (GINA Track 1, ICS-formoterol):

| Step | Therapy |
|---|---|
| 1-2 | Low-dose ICS-formoterol as needed |
| 3 | Low-dose ICS-formoterol maintenance + as-needed |
| 4 | Medium-dose ICS-formoterol maintenance + as-needed |
| 5 | High-dose ICS-formoterol + add-on (LAMA, biologic if eligible) |

**§6.3. Inhaler technique** check at every annual review — half of patients use their inhaler incorrectly.

**§6.4. Peak flow monitoring.** Personal best PEF assessed once over 2 weeks; calculate green/amber/red zones at 80% and 60% of personal best.

## §7. Cross-cutting principles

**§7.1. BP measurement technique.** Seated, back supported, feet on floor, arm at heart level, after 5 minutes' rest. Two readings 1 minute apart; record the second. Single-arm difference >20 systolic warrants vascular evaluation.

**§7.2. Drug-drug interactions of note in primary care:**

- **ACE-i + spironolactone** — hyperkalaemia risk; check K⁺ at 1 week then 4 weeks.
- **Metformin + iodinated contrast** — hold 48 h around contrast study if eGFR <60.
- **SSRI + tramadol** — serotonin syndrome risk; prefer codeine or alternative.
- **Statin + clarithromycin / itraconazole** — myopathy risk; hold statin during 7-day course.
- **Warfarin + antibiotics** (especially metronidazole, ciprofloxacin) — recheck INR at day 3.

**§7.3. Pre-visit blood draw timing.** Schedule HbA1c, lipid, eGFR for 1-2 weeks before the follow-up visit so results are ready in time.
